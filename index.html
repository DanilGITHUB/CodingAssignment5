Deviled Eggs

A classic appetizer — adapted from U.S.A. Cookbook by Sheila Lukins

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1–2 dashes Tabasco sauce, to taste
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp snipped fresh chives
  • 3 tbsp mayonnaise
  • Paprika, for garnish
  • Whole fresh chives, for garnish

Equipment

  • Small saucepan
  • Tablespoon
  • Fork
  • Knife
  • Platter

Directions

  1. Boil: Rinse eggs with warm water, and place in a small saucepan. Cover with cold water, place the pan over medium-high heat and bring to a boil. Turn off heat, cover and let sit for 10–15 minutes. Drain, rinse under cold water and peel. Cool in the refrigerator, loosely covered, for 15 minutes.
  2. Mix Filling: Halve eggs lengthwise, and carefully scoop out yolks. Place yolks in a bowl, and mash with a fork. Add mustard, Tabasco, salt, pepper and snipped chives. Stir in mayonnaise.
  3. Fill & Garnish: Fill each egg white with about 1½ teaspoons of the egg-yolk mixture and dust the top with paprika. Arrange in a spoke design on a platter; garnish with whole chives.
